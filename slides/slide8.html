Risk Management - Core Concepts

What is Risk Management?

  • Organizational process to identify, analyze, and control risks to systems.
  • Goal: Minimize risks to acceptable levels while supporting business needs.

Key Concepts

  • Risk: Combination of threat likelihood and impact.
  • Threat: Potential cause of an unwanted incident.
  • Vulnerability: Weakness that can be exploited by a threat.
  • Exploit: Attack that takes advantage of a vulnerability.

Sources of Uncertainty

  • Who is the attacker?
  • What is their goal?
  • How likely is the attack to succeed?
  • What will the impact be if successful?

Risk Assessment

  • Risk Management: Organizational control to risk.
  • Risk Assessment: Identify, Analyze, and Evaluate risk.
  • Risk Analysis: Helps understand risk severity and needed action.

Qualitative Risk Assessment

  • Based on experience, intuition, and judgment.
  • Techniques: Delphi method, Brainstorming, Storyboarding, Surveys.

Quantitative Risk Assessment

  • Assigns numbers to probabilities and impacts.
  • Measures:
    • Asset Value (AV) - Worth of an asset.
    • Exposure Factor (EF) - % of loss if a threat materializes.
    • Single Loss Expectancy (SLE) = AV × EF.
    • Annualized Rate of Occurrence (ARO) - Expected number of incidents per year.
    • Annualized Loss Expectancy (ALE) = SLE × ARO.

Risk Treatment Options

  • Reduce (mitigate)
  • Transfer (insurance)
  • Avoid (eliminate activity)
  • Accept (live with the risk)

Risk Visualization: Risk Matrix

  • Impact (Low, Mid, High, Extreme) on one axis.
  • Likelihood (Very Low, Low, Mid, High) on the other axis.
  • Risks are categorized to prioritize actions.

Other Concepts

  • Residual Risk: Risk remaining after controls are applied.
  • Incident Management: Steps to handle and recover from security incidents.
